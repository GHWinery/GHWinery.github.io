# Safety Guidelines

!!! danger "Critical Information"
    All team members must read and understand these safety guidelines before beginning work. Safety is everyone's responsibility.

## General Safety Rules

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

**Required at all times in production areas:**

- Closed-toe, non-slip shoes
- Long pants (no shorts)
- Safety glasses when operating equipment
- Hearing protection in designated areas

**Additional PPE when specified:**

- Chemical-resistant gloves
- Face shields
- Respirators (fit-tested personnel only)
- Hard hats in designated zones

### Prohibited Items

The following are not permitted in production areas:

- Open-toed shoes or sandals
- Loose jewelry
- Long, unrestrained hair
- Flowing clothing
- Mobile phones (except designated areas)

## Chemical Safety

### Handling Chemicals

!!! warning "Chemical Hazards"
    Many winemaking chemicals can cause serious injury if mishandled.

**Before using any chemical:**

1. Read the Safety Data Sheet (SDS)
2. Wear appropriate PPE
3. Ensure adequate ventilation
4. Know the location of eyewash stations and safety showers

### Common Chemicals

| Chemical | Hazards | PPE Required |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂) | Respiratory irritant, toxic gas | Respirator, gloves, goggles |
| Caustic Soda (NaOH) | Corrosive, burns | Gloves, face shield, apron |
| Citric Acid | Irritant | Gloves, safety glasses |
| Tartaric Acid | Irritant | Gloves, safety glasses |
| Peracetic Acid | Corrosive, oxidizer | Gloves, goggles, apron |

### Chemical Storage

- Store chemicals in designated areas only
- Keep incompatible chemicals separated
- Ensure proper labeling and dating
- Never store above head height
- Keep MSDS/SDS readily accessible

### Spill Response

**Small Spills:**

1. Alert nearby personnel
2. Put on appropriate PPE
3. Use spill kit materials
4. Dispose of waste properly
5. Report to supervisor

**Large Spills or Unknown Substances:**

1. Evacuate the area
2. Alert management immediately
3. Do not attempt cleanup
4. Call emergency services if needed

## Equipment Safety

### Before Operating Equipment

- Complete required training
- Inspect equipment for damage
- Ensure all guards are in place
- Verify emergency stops are functional
- Remove jewelry and secure loose clothing/hair

### Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)

!!! danger "Energy Isolation"
    Never service equipment without proper lockout/tagout procedures.

**LOTO is required for:**

- Any maintenance or repair work
- Cleaning inside tanks or vessels
- Clearing jams or blockages
- Any work on energized equipment

**Authorized personnel only** - LOTO training required

### Common Equipment Hazards

**Crush/Destemmer:**

- Pinch points
- Rotating augers
- Noise levels >85dB

**Presses:**

- High pressure hydraulics
- Crushing hazards
- Moving parts

**Forklifts:**

- Pedestrian traffic
- Load stability
- Visibility limitations
- Certified operators only

**Pumps:**

- Hot liquids
- High pressure
- Chemical exposure

## Confined Space Entry

### Confined Spaces Include:

- Fermentation tanks
- Storage tanks
- Barrel rooms during harvest
- Pits and sumps

!!! danger "Permit Required"
    Never enter a confined space without a valid entry permit and trained attendant.

### Entry Requirements

1. Valid confined space entry permit
2. Atmospheric testing completed
3. Ventilation system operating
4. Trained attendant posted
5. Rescue equipment ready
6. Communication system tested

### Atmospheric Hazards

- **CO₂ accumulation** - Displaces oxygen, especially in fermentation tanks
- **Oxygen deficiency** - Can cause unconsciousness
- **Flammable vapors** - Alcohol vapors during fermentation
- **Toxic gases** - Hydrogen sulfide, SO₂

## Ergonomics and Manual Handling

### Lifting Safely

**Proper lifting technique:**

1. Assess the load first
2. Keep back straight
3. Bend at knees, not waist
4. Hold load close to body
5. Lift with legs
6. Don't twist while carrying

**Weight Limits:**

- Maximum single-person lift: 50 lbs
- Team lift for anything heavier
- Use mechanical aids when available

### Repetitive Tasks

- Take regular breaks
- Rotate tasks when possible
- Report discomfort early
- Use proper posture
- Utilize ergonomic tools

## Slip, Trip, and Fall Prevention

### Keep Areas Clean

- Wipe up spills immediately
- Keep walkways clear
- Store equipment properly
- Use caution signs for wet floors
- Report damaged flooring

### Working at Heights

- Use appropriate ladders or platforms
- Three points of contact on ladders
- Never work alone at heights
- Use fall protection when required
- Inspect ladders before use

## Electrical Safety

!!! danger "Electrical Hazards"
    Contact with electricity can be fatal.

### Safe Practices

- Keep electrical panels clear
- Never use damaged cords or equipment
- Keep electrical equipment away from water
- Use GFCI protection
- Report exposed wiring immediately

### During Wet Cleanup

- Use GFCI-protected circuits
- Keep power cords elevated
- Never spray water on electrical equipment
- De-energize circuits when possible

## Fire Safety

### Fire Prevention

- Keep combustible materials away from ignition sources
- Properly store flammable liquids
- Don't overload electrical outlets
- Keep fire exits and extinguishers clear
- No smoking in facility

### Fire Extinguisher Use (PASS)

1. **P**ull the pin
2. **A**im at the base of the fire
3. **S**queeze the handle
4. **S**weep side to side

### Evacuation Procedures

**If fire alarm sounds:**

1. Stop work immediately
2. Evacuate using nearest safe exit
3. Assist others if safe to do so
4. Assemble at designated meeting point
5. Report to supervisor for headcount
6. Do not re-enter until authorized

**Assembly Points:**

- Primary: North parking lot
- Secondary: East lawn area

## Emergency Procedures

### Medical Emergencies

1. Call 911 for serious injuries
2. Alert supervisor/manager
3. Provide first aid if trained
4. Do not move seriously injured persons
5. Complete incident report

### Chemical Exposure

**Eye Contact:**

1. Flush with eyewash for 15 minutes
2. Hold eyelids open
3. Seek medical attention

**Skin Contact:**

1. Remove contaminated clothing
2. Flush with water for 15 minutes
3. Use safety shower if large area
4. Seek medical attention

**Inhalation:**

1. Move to fresh air
2. Seek medical attention immediately
3. Do not re-enter area

**Ingestion:**

1. Do not induce vomiting
2. Seek medical attention immediately
3. Bring SDS/container if possible

### Injury Reporting

**All injuries must be reported,** no matter how minor:

1. Notify supervisor immediately
2. Seek medical attention if needed
3. Complete incident report within 24 hours
4. Participate in investigation if requested

## Alcohol and Substance Policy

- No alcohol consumption during work hours
- Sensory evaluation in designated areas only
- Expectoration required for all tastings
- No illegal substances tolerated
- Medication that impairs must be reported
